# New Leader: The First Six Months

> **Edition:** 1st Edition

---

## Introduction

- During this period, one-on-ones are extremely important. Unlike my regular one-on-ones, which are usually just open conversation, I structure these much more deliberately.

- Inform the leader about what I expect of them: It's no longer just about developing code — it's about building the team and helping them succeed.

- **What does it mean to succeed as a Tech Lead?**
  - Many think it means doing more — that's not necessarily the case. It's about finding a balance between succeeding as a leader for others and as an employee for your own leaders.
  - It's about structuring the work so that it becomes transparent, and you're not just driving on one side of the road (in the ditch).
  - It's about motivating the people on the team.
  - It's about filtering the right messages to your people.
  - It's about saying no to unrealistic requests from others, while being clear about why — and offering alternative solutions.
  - It's about not just saying no, but also coming up with a solution.
  - It's about communication.
  - It's about showing up to more activities. For example, all-hands meetings.
  - Visualize that you are delivering (Use the Dunning-Kruger effect).

  - **ALL OF THIS** leads to building trust with both your team and leadership. Is it measurable? Maybe.

- **Things to remember:**
  - You are now a leader both during and outside of working hours.
  - You gain more freedom, but also significantly more responsibility.

## Phase 1: Onboarding

### Month 1: Introduction (Complete)

**Intro:**
This will be a tough month. You need to familiarize yourself with all the responsibilities the previous person held. This includes meetings, discussions, reporting, and more.

**Monthly Task: Meeting Structure and Rhythm**
- Which recurring meetings does the frontend team participate in? (standups, sprint meetings, demos, retros)
- Which meetings should they attend as a leader? (leadership team, stakeholder meetings, planning)
- How much time is spent on meetings vs. development within the team?
- Which meetings are perceived as valuable vs. time-wasters?

**First One-on-One with Their Direct Reports:**
- Keep it very unstructured (removes the pressure of being the perfect leader).
- Start with something as simple as "How are you doing?"
- You are now this person's leader, but both of you share the responsibility to discuss challenges and solutions.
- What do your direct reports expect from you? (Let them think about it until next time.)
- Introduce the Grandmother Rule with your reports.
- Try to find one thing you can help your reports with (action points)! This gives them a concrete way to create value and start building trust.

**Grandmother Rule:**
- 2 positive things, 1 challenge.

**Closing Words:**
- Don't change too much at once — and this is going to be fine!
- If you have challenges or just want to discuss something, don't wait until the next one-on-one — I always have 20 minutes!

### Month 2: Team Dynamics

**Intro:**
Now that you have an overview of the meeting structure and operational routines, it's time to understand the people you'll be leading. This month is about mapping the team dynamics — both the formal and informal structures that affect how the team functions.

**Review Tasks from Previous Meeting:**
- Meeting structure and rhythm, team status & review Grandmother Rule.

**Monthly Task: Map Team Dynamics**
- Who are the informal leaders? How does information flow? Where are the bottlenecks in collaboration?
- Who are the key people and what are their strengths/development areas?
- Where are the knowledge gaps in the team?
- How does collaboration work internally and with other teams?
- Who goes to whom when they need help?
- Implement one concrete improvement in team collaboration based on your findings (can be as simple as a new communication routine or a new meeting format).

**Closing Words:**
- Remember that team dynamics isn't just about observing — you influence it through your presence. Be mindful of how your role as a new leader changes existing patterns.
- If you have challenges or just want to discuss something, don't wait until the next one-on-one — I always have 20 minutes!

---

### Month 3: Technical Assessment

**Intro:**
You now have a good overview of operations and the people on the team. Now it's time to dive deep into the technical state. This month is about understanding the technology, processes, and quality of what you deliver — and most importantly, prioritizing what needs to be fixed first.

**Review Tasks from Previous Meeting:**
- Team dynamics mapping, team status & review Grandmother Rule.
- What concrete improvement did you implement in team collaboration?
- Grandmother Rule: 2 positive things, 1 challenge.

**Monthly Task: Map Technical State**
- Map technical debt: Where are the biggest pain points in the codebase?
- Performance and quality: What metrics do you have, and where do you stand?
- Technology stack: What works well? What should be considered for upgrade/replacement?
- Testing and deployment: How robust are the processes?
- Create a prioritized list of technical debt and get buy-in from the team on the top 3.

**Closing Words:**
- Technical debt can feel overwhelming when you map everything. Focus on impact vs. effort — what provides the most value to fix first?
- Involve the team in prioritization — they often know best where the shoe pinches in daily work.
- If you have challenges or just want to discuss something, don't wait until the next one-on-one — I always have 20 minutes!

---

### Month 4: Strategy and Communication

**Intro:**
You now know the team, the processes, and the technical state. Now it's time to think strategically — how will you move forward? This month is about creating a plan that balances technical needs with business goals, and learning to communicate technical decisions to people who don't code.

**Review Tasks from Previous Meeting:**
- Technical state mapping & review Grandmother Rule.
- Did you get buy-in from the team on the top 3 technical debt items?

**Monthly Task: Strategy and Communication**
- Create a 3-month technical roadmap for the frontend team based on business priorities.
- How do you communicate technical decisions to non-technical stakeholders?
- Hold one meeting with stakeholders where you present technical priorities.

**Closing Words:**
- Good communication with stakeholders isn't about simplifying everything — it's about focusing on consequences and alternatives they can understand.
- Remember that roadmaps are living documents — the most important thing is that everyone understands the direction and the reasoning.
- If you have challenges or just want to discuss something, don't wait until the next one-on-one — I always have 20 minutes!

---

### Month 5: Team Development

**Intro:**
You have now built a foundation as a leader — overview, relationships, and strategy. Now it's time to focus on what often gets forgotten during busy periods: developing the people you lead. This month is about understanding the team's ambitions and building concrete plans for their future.

**Review Tasks from Previous Meeting:**
- 3-month roadmap & review Grandmother Rule.
- How did the stakeholder meeting on technical priorities go?

**Monthly Task: Team Development**
- Hold development conversations with each of your direct reports: What are their wishes for the future? (career, technology, responsibilities)
- Create an individual development plan for each person based on their goals and the team's needs.

**Closing Words:**
- People often want more than just a raise — autonomy, learning, and recognition can be just as important.
- A good development plan balances the individual's wishes with the team's and company's needs — not everyone can become a tech lead at the same time.
- If you have challenges or just want to discuss something, don't wait until the next one-on-one — I always have 20 minutes!

---

### Month 6: Reflection and the Road Ahead

**Intro:**
Congratulations — you've made it through the first six months as a leader! This period has been about building the foundation for your leadership — from the operational to the strategic, from team dynamics to individual development. Now it's time to reflect on the journey so far and set the course for the future.

**Review Tasks from Previous Meeting:**
- Development conversations and individual development plans & review Grandmother Rule.

**Monthly Task: Reflection and the Road Ahead**
- Conduct a 360-degree evaluation: Gather feedback from team members, peers, and other stakeholders.
- What have you learned about yourself as a leader? (strengths, blind spots, communication style)
- Which areas of the leadership role feel natural, and which are still challenging?
- Create your own leadership development plan for the next six months with concrete goals.
- Set up a system for continuous feedback within the team (not just during formal evaluations).

**Closing Words:**
- The first six months are about learning the job — the next six are about making it your own.
- Leadership is not a destination, but a continuous journey of learning and adaptation.
- The most important thing is not to avoid mistakes, but to learn from them and adjust course.
- You now have both the tools and the experience to take ownership of your leadership role — the time to fly on your own has come!
- I'm still here for you when you need me, but now you're the one setting the agenda!
